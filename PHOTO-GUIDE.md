# Photographing knives for the website

Good, consistent photos are the single biggest thing that will make the site
look professional. The site also **removes the background automatically**, and
that works *far* better when the photo is shot on a plain surface.

A phone camera is totally fine — it's about the setup, not the gear.

## The 60-second setup
1. **Plain background.** Lay the knife on a sheet of plain **white or light‑grey
   paper / card**, or a plain dark cloth. A large sheet of paper curved up
   behind the knife (so there's no hard line) is ideal.
2. **Good light.** Shoot near a **window in daytime**, light coming from the
   side. Avoid the camera flash. Avoid harsh direct sun (blows out the blade).
3. **No clutter.** Nothing else in the frame — no tools, hands, keys, patterned
   cloths.
4. **Fill the frame.** Get close so the knife fills most of the picture, lying
   **horizontally**, with a little space around it.
5. **Same angle every time.** Either straight‑down (top view) or a slight
   3/4 angle — but keep it **consistent** across all knives so the store grid
   looks tidy.

## Do / Don't
| Do | Don't |
| --- | --- |
| Plain paper / card / solid cloth | Wood grain, driftwood, cutting boards |
| Daytime window light | Camera flash, harsh sun |
| Clean the lens first | Busy or patterned backgrounds |
| Knife horizontal, filling the frame | Tiny knife lost in a big frame |
| Wipe fingerprints off the blade | Cluttered table / other objects |

> The 4 photos that didn't cut out well on launch (the karambits and a couple of
> skinners) were shot on wood / patterned surfaces — re‑shooting those on plain
> paper will fix them.

## Tips for a clean phone photo
- Tap the knife on screen to focus, then hold to **lock focus/exposure**.
- Turn the **flash off**; use window light instead.
- Take 2–3 shots and keep the sharpest.
- A second close‑up of the handle or any engraving is a nice extra (the product
  page shows additional photos as thumbnails).

## Uploading
Add the photo in the CMS (the **Knives** or **Gallery** section). The site
generates the cut‑out version automatically when it next builds — no editing
needed on your side.
